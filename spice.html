Miami Spice

So far, I've dined at Essensia for dinner, L'echon Brasserie for lunch, Morton's The Steakhouse for dinner (by accident), and I plan to go to Hakkasan for dim sum lunch as soon as I can actually get a reservation.

  1. Essensia

    2. Dinner was good, but it didn't blow my mind. The steak melted in my mouth, and I liked the mango chutney that accompanied the Indian-inspired chicken dish. However, dessert tasted like a condensed frozen Thai coconut lemongrass soup, which is confusing to say the least.

  2. L'echon Brasserie

    3. The Pubbelly Group never disappoints. In each of the dishes, the flavor of each ingredient stood out just as it should: the leeks in the Scottish salmon dish, the cinnamon-y flavor of the pizza-like ham tart.

  3. Morton's The Steakhouse

    4. First things first, under ordinary circumstances, I would never eat at a chain restaurant for Miami Spice. But I went to Morton's for dinner with my family because my dad had gift cards; we didn't even realize there was a Miami Spice menu until the server brought it to our table. I ordered the filet mignon -- because who's going to go to a steakhouse and not eat steak? -- and actually thought it was worth it.
